Why TeamUp

😖

Today: WhatsApp chaos

Every student needs a 4-person team under strict rules (grade mix, branch, gender). Finding teammates over group chats is messy, and you can't tell if a team is even valid until it's too late.

✅

TeamUp: rules that enforce themselves

An invalid team is impossible to form. Live filters, a searchable directory, faculty mentors, and an AI assistant — all on one page, hosted on the college server.

A guided walkthrough

Getting in & finding a team

1

Secure sign-in

Students log in with their SRN. Registration is confirmed by an email OTP, and forgotten passwords reset the same way.

  • Salted scrypt password hashing
  • Email-verified accounts, unique per email
  • Multi-device sessions
2

One profile, everything captured

Name, SRN, branch, gender and CGPA at signup — plus an email for recovery and an optional WhatsApp number so teammates can reach out.

  • CGPA becomes a private A/B/C grade to others
  • Branch drives who you can team with
3

Browse teams looking for members

Every open team on one screen, filtered by branch, domain and open grade slot. Cards show live seat counts and exactly which grades still fit.

  • "Eligible to join" shows only teams you can join
  • Grade-slot logic enforces the allowed combos

A guided walkthrough

Finding people & forming the team

4

Search the whole batch

An A→Z directory of every student — see who's still free, filter by branch, gender, grade or domain interest, and request to join their team on the spot.

  • Runs in SQL — instant even at 1,500 students
  • Interests & contact shown inline
5

Pick the right mentor

All 58 EC-campus faculty with photos, emails and domain expertise — searchable by name or domain, so you find a guide whose interests match your project.

  • Parsed from the official faculty-domains sheet
  • One click to attach a mentor to your team
6

Run your team

The leader adds a description and mentor; members are listed with grades and GitHub. Leadership can be transferred, members removed, and open slots shown live.

  • Any member can accept requests & invite students
  • Kick / disband / transfer are leader-only

Built to launch

Engineered for the whole batch

⚡ Load-tested

1,600 students / 350 teams — 20–40 ms/request; 200 concurrent requests served in ~3 s.

🛡️ Crash-safe

SQLite + WAL, ACID transactions. Verified with kill -9 mid-write — zero data loss.

💾 Backed up

Nightly SQLite snapshots, 14-day retention. One file to copy, restore, or hand off.

Why SQLite, not MongoDB

No database server to host

  • It's a file, not a service — nothing for IT to install, expose a port for, or maintain
  • Real relational SQL — tables, joins, ACID transactions
  • Portable further — exports to MySQL / PostgreSQL with zero rework
Tech stack
  • Node.js + Express — one service, no build step
  • SQLite (better-sqlite3) — one indexed file
  • Vanilla HTML / CSS / JS frontend
  • Ollama + Qwen for the assistant (CPU, no GPU)
  • Docker image · systemd · Caddy HTTPS
