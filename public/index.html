Hello there 👋 Welcome to TeamUp

A portal made to form teams for Capstone 2026–28.

Here, you can connect with students from other sections who share your academic goals and interests — form a new team or join an existing one based on the academic and gender criteria. You can also browse and find the right project mentor whose expertise aligns with the domain you choose.

This website has all the required criteria built in, which will help you filter out students who are eligible for your team — or the team you would want to join.

🔁 Overall process

1 · Team formation 2 · Guide allocation 3 · Continuous assessment by a panel (ISA + ESA) 4 · Outcome publications

👥 Team formation rules

  • You may form your batch yourself — exactly 4 students from across the computing branches; CSE and AIML students can combine to form groups.
  • Academic diversity (mandatory): no group may have more than two students from the same grade category, and every group must include at least one member from category C (CGPA below 7).
  • Gender diversity (preferable): each batch should preferably include at least one member of a different gender — but all-male / all-female teams are allowed.

✅ Allowed grade combinations

A A B C A B B C A B C C A A C C B B C C

❌ Not allowed

A A B B — or any other combination

A = CGPA 8.00 and above · B = 7.00–7.99 · C = 6.99 and below.

🧑‍🏫 Step 2 — find a guide

  • You may choose any domain and problem statement based on your team's interest.
  • A list of faculty members and their domains is shared — faculty domains sheet — and you can connect with mentors right here on TeamUp who align with your domain and academic interests.
  • Talk to faculty in the department with interests matching your team, and discuss problem statements over mail — they will give inputs and suggestions.
  • After discussion with faculty, each batch proposes a guide.
